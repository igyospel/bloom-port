# Bloomport Competitor Analysis
**Last Updated:** May 26, 2026  
**Competitors Analyzed:** calm.com, headspace.com, woebot.io, reflectly.app, wysa.com, youper.ai  
**Purpose:** Identify keyword gaps, content weaknesses, and Bloomport's competitive advantages

---

## Executive Summary

Bloomport's core competitive advantage is the **free + AI + web-based** combination that none of these competitors offer simultaneously. All major competitors are either:
- App-store only (not web-first)
- Paid or freemium with aggressive paywalls
- Not AI-powered in a meaningful way
- Not targeting the productivity/focus angle alongside mindfulness

Bloomport sits at the intersection of these gaps. This analysis maps exactly where to attack.

### Content Gaps (What Calm Does NOT Cover Well)

- **AI-specific content:** Calm has zero content on AI-powered mental health tools. Their blog is entirely human-expert articles.
- **Free tool comparisons:** They never write "best free meditation tools" because they're not free.
- **Productivity/focus integration:** Calm is purely wellness — they never connect to work productivity or Pomodoro-style tools.
- **ADHD/executive function content:** Almost no content targeting neurodivergent users.
- **Technical anxiety (work stress, decision fatigue):** Their content is emotion-focused, not situation-specific.
- **Budget-conscious users:** No content targeting "free" seekers — they push paid app.

### Format Patterns
- Long-form blog articles (2,000-4,000 words)
- Heavy on expert quotes (clinical psychologists, sleep researchers)
- Rarely publishes comparison or roundup content
- No interactive tools on blog pages

### Bloomport Can Beat Calm For:
- "free meditation app alternative" — Calm never writes this, Bloomport can own it
- "AI meditation tool" — Calm has no AI angle at all
- Any keyword with "free" in it — Calm's SEO avoids "free" language
